Вид: $\int R(\sin x, \cos x)dx$
### Универсальная тригонометрическая подстановка
$t=\tan \frac{x}{2}$
$$\cos x = \frac{1-t^{2}}{1+t^{2}}, \ \sin x = \frac{2t}{1+t^{2}}, \ \ dx=\frac{2dt}{1+t^{2}} \to \int \tilde{R}(t)dt$$
Пример: $\int \frac{dx}{3-\sin x+2\cos x}$
$$3-\sin x+2\cos x=3- \frac{2t}{1+t^{2}} + 2 \frac{1-t^{2}}{1+t^{2}}= \frac{3+3t^{2}-2t+2-2t^{2}}{1+t^{2}}= \frac{t^{2}-2t+5}{1+t^{2}}$$
$$I(t)=\int \frac{2dt}{1+t^{2}} \cdot \frac{t^{2}+1}{t^{2}-2t+5}=2 \int \frac{dt}{(t-1)^{2}+ 4}=\frac{2}{2} \arctan( \frac{t-1}{2})+C=$$
$$I(x)=\arctan \frac{ \tan \frac{x}{2}  - 1}{2} +C$$
